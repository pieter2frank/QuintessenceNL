Quintessence Fundraising

In spite of a Managing Director’s extensive network and a Communication Department’s talented writers, fundraising is never a simple activity and requires a great deal of energy and know-how. In our opinion, fundraising should never be the sole responsibility of a single staff member, no matter how skilled this individual may be. Rather, fundraising is achieved through the effective coordination of all the various departments within an organization. An effective fundraising coordinator is able to oversee these activities, manage campaigns and acts as a catalyst of ideas. This is where our team is able to help. Some examples:

FUNDRAISING IS ABOUT MORE THAN JUST MONEY …

Fundraising is about more than just the money, however important this money may be. First and foremost, fundraising needs to take into account long-term planning and results: building a good rapport with sponsors, donors, corporate businesses, organizations and foundations. A positive relationship can last for years if based on commitment and transparency. In other words, investing in donors and meeting their needs and wishes is paramount. This requires creativity, and integrity in dealing with people. It is our pleasure to assist you in achieving this.

EVENTS AND CAMPAIGNS

When the timing is right, participating in large public events provides great opportunities to acquire new donors as well as to create a lot of free publicity for your organization or cause. Another type of event is one which is organised specifically with your cause or charity in mind, for example, a sponsored sports event. There are many corporate businesses prepared to join a good cause.

CONCERTS BY POPULAR ARTISTS

Effective fundraising requires activities and campaigns that meet the needs of particular target groups. For example, youth can be reached through concerts by popular artists and musicians whose work appeals to that particular age group. Courage and perseverance can open up many possibilities and bring about results.

CLASSICAL CONCERTS

An intimate performance of chamber music, a large orchestra with choir, an ensemble or a soloist: we have the means to organise any variety of classical concert. In any case, this type of event is able to draw a specific target group with an interest in culture and, in many cases, with the necessary means to support your goals. Classical concerts are particularly suitable for corporate sponsorship.

GALA EVENINGS

Gala evenings and dinners are valuable meeting opportunities to honour large donors and to add other important, invited guests to your existing database. Corporate businesses and captains of industry are often willing increase their visibility in such spheres and to make significant contributions to your charity or cause.

GIFTS IN KIND

Companies are increasingly willing to add sponsorship through ‘gifts in kind’. Not all causes are able to benefit directly from such in kind sponsorship, due to logistical reasons for instance, however, there are several alternatives. In a straightforward example, in kind donations can be given away as prizes in competitions. The multitude of sponsored television programmes provides another clear example.

TESTAMENTS AND LEGACIES

Long term fundraising efforts require clear policies surrounding final will and testaments and legacies. Although often regarded as a delicate issue, with adequate and considerate policies in place, their long term relevance cannot be overlooked. For instance, as the generation of so-called baby boomers age, they are faced increasingly with questions surrounding their legacies and final wishes. It is important to be able to react suitably to this societal development.
