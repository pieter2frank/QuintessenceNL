Quintessence has become officially Climate Neutral

Since June 2011 Quintessence has become officially Climate Neutral. Together with the Fair Climate Fund we made a CO2 emission scan for the first half year of 2011. The total emission has been calculated and compensated by the purchase of verified Carbon Credits of a Biogas facility in India. From now on this calculation will take place every half year. A part of the CO2 emission comes from the energy consumption in the office, but a bigger part is the result of business trips by car, airplane or other types of transport. Beside the business emission, the private emission has been calculated and compensated as well.

Fair Climate Fund is building biogas facilities in the Kolar District, in the State of Karnataka, India. This is one of the least developed regions in India. The dirty streets and houses, in combination with cooking at an open fire, cause enormous health problems. 18.000 families will shortly be able to cook in a clean and climate neutral manner. Fair Climate Fund and the Agricultural Development and Training Society (ADATS) are presently working very hard to build this alternative cooking method of 18.000 biogas installations.

These facilities can produce enough methane gas to cook every day. There is no cost involved for the poor families. Now, the cow crap that used to be thrown away, is placed in the facility together with other organic residues, producing biogas that gives three times more heat than firewood. The direct benefits of this is a reduction of CO2 emissions, less fuel cost, les lumber, healthier living conditions, a decrease in waste treatment costs, and an enormous saving in time. A secondary effect is the increase in job opportunities created by building and maintaining the facilities.

Henk Koppelaar: “We do believe in sustainable management and environmental protection wherever possible. Therefore we are proud that all activities of Quintessence from now on will have no further impact on the climate.”

WNF first organization with 4 stars in the Charity Checklist ©

The World Wildlife Fund in The Netherlands - WNF is the first organization in Holland reaching the rank of 4 stars in the Charity Checklist. The content and layout of the 2009/2010 Annual report, the openness about (financial) results and failures and the way WNF used all kind of other communication platforms to spread there message, are a sample for many other charities in this sector. In order to objectively provide a qualitative ranking system for charity organizations, Quintessence has developed a Charity Checklist ©. Our research methodology is based on a carefully compiled series of questions, with over 200 criteria used to evaluate a charity organization. For each criterion, the organization is awarded a certain number of points. The total number of points awarded in those criteria (maximum of 600) determines the allocation of stars (*). A max. number of 5 stars can be awarded.

Pakistan 6 months after the floods

It has been six months since devastating floods swept through Pakistan in late July 2010, inundating large swathes of the country and causing destruction on a massive scale. Triggered by torrential rains, the floods washed away crops, submerged and destroyed villages, and caused an estimated 1,700 deaths. Read more in the latest report of Doctors without Borders. MSF
